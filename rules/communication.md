# Communication & Writing Rules

## Email Drafting
- Under 200 words. Executives scan, they don't read.
- Start with a specific fact, reference, or number — never a pleasantry
- One specific ask per email with a suggested date/time
- Match the recipient's seniority:
  - C-suite: strategic, concise, bold claims with data
  - VP/Director: tactical, outcome-focused, specific timelines
  - Technical: precise product names, architecture-aware, honest about tradeoffs

## Slack Messages
- Keep under 3 sentences for channel messages
- Use threads for detail
- Lead with the action or decision, not context
- Bold the key takeaway in longer messages
- Never post walls of text — use bullets or blocks

## Customer-Facing vs Internal
- Customer-facing: 70% their world, 30% Salesforce. Minimize product names. Lead with their metrics.
- Internal: be candid. Use product names freely. Include deal details, competitive intel.
- Never send an internal message externally or vice versa. Double-check the audience.

## Banned Patterns
- "I hope this finds you well" — start with substance
- "Per our conversation" — just reference what was discussed
- "Please don't hesitate to reach out" — give a specific next step
- "As discussed" without specifics — say what was discussed
- "Circling back" — just state the update
- "Wanted to touch base" — state the purpose

## Anti-Slop (Active in ALL Content)
Every piece of content must pass the slop test:
- No banned words (see CLAUDE.md)
- No AI-sounding sentence structures
- No generic filler — every sentence earns its place
- If removing a sentence doesn't change the meaning, remove it
